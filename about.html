Origin

A meme as an entry point, not a ceiling

When Bitcoin launched in 2009, most people who heard about it thought it was a joke. Digital money that existed only on the internet, invented by someone using a pseudonym, with no government backing and no central authority? Absurd. A decade later it was a trillion-dollar asset class.

Dogecoin began as a literal meme — a Shiba Inu dog on a bright yellow background, launched in 2013 as a parody. It became one of the most widely recognised cryptocurrency projects in the world and its community-first culture became a template countless projects copied.

We are not claiming TrustTails will follow either trajectory. That comparison exists to illustrate one thing: a meme is a format, not a destiny. The format gets attention. What you do with that attention is everything. TrustTails started as a meme to be noticed. Everything that comes after has to be earned, not claimed.

The name holds the philosophy: Trust is the thing the crypto industry has a documented problem building. Tails is the other side of the coin — the risk, the uncertainty, the long road — the stuff most projects hide. We show you both sides. That is the point.

Why the format is honest

A meme coin carries zero inherited prestige. It cannot borrow credibility from an institutional backer or a famous founder. Every bit of trust must be built from scratch, in public, through verifiable on-chain evidence. That constraint is not a weakness — it is the most honest starting point available in crypto right now.

TAIL Network

Why build an independent chain — and what does that actually mean?

TAIL Network is being built on the Cosmos SDK. Here is what that means in plain language — and what we will not claim about it.

What the Cosmos SDK is

The Cosmos SDK is an open-source framework for building independent, application-specific blockchains. It is not Solana. It is not Ethereum. Projects that build on it create their own sovereign chains — they set their own rules, control their own upgrades, and are not dependent on the decisions of another network's validators or developers.

Why a project might choose it

Sovereignty. A token on Solana exists inside Solana's ecosystem — its future is partly determined by decisions Solana's developers make. An independent Cosmos SDK chain controls its own logic, economics, and upgrade path. That is the appeal: no dependency on another network's roadmap. This is the direction TAIL Network is oriented toward — in development.

What we will not claim about it

TAIL Network is in development. It does not have a live mainnet, confirmed validators, a chain ID, an explorer, staking parameters, or a confirmed launch date. We will not invent those details. When TAIL Network reaches milestones, they will be announced publicly — not back-filled into a page that made them up in advance.
