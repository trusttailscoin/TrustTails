STRAIGHT ANSWERS

TrustTails (TAIL) FAQ

No hype, no spin. Every question answered with exactly what we know — and what we don't. If something here changes, we'll update this page and announce it through our official channels.

THE BASICS

What TrustTails is — and isn't

What is TrustTails?

TrustTails is a Solana-based SPL token with the ticker TAIL. It started as a meme — which is an honest starting point for most tokens. The difference is that everything after that has to be earned, not claimed. The project's thesis is that credibility in crypto is built through verifiable transparency, not promises. The total supply is fixed at 1,000,000,000 TAIL, and both the mint and freeze authorities have been permanently revoked.

You can read the full background on our About page or download the whitepaper (PDF).

What is the ticker symbol and which blockchain is it on?

The ticker is TAIL. TrustTails is deployed on Solana as an SPL token. Solana was chosen for its speed, low transaction fees, and the maturity of its token infrastructure. See our Why Solana? page for more detail.

What is the total supply — and will it ever change?

The total supply is exactly 1,000,000,000 TAIL (one billion). It will never change. The mint authority — the permission needed to create new tokens — has been permanently revoked on-chain. This is not a promise; it is a done, irreversible action. No person, team, or smart contract can mint additional TAIL.

You can verify this directly on Solscan: Solscan token page. Look for "Mint Authority" — it will show as revoked.

What does "mint authority revoked" actually mean?

On Solana, an SPL token is created with an optional "mint authority" — an account that can issue new tokens at any time. If that authority is still active, the team could inflate the supply without warning. Revoking the mint authority removes this ability permanently. Once revoked, the on-chain record cannot be undone. The supply is mathematically capped at 1,000,000,000 TAIL forever.

What does "freeze authority revoked" mean?

The freeze authority is a separate permission that, if active, lets the token issuer freeze individual wallets — preventing those holders from transferring or selling. This is a significant power that can be abused. TrustTails has permanently revoked the freeze authority. No wallet holding TAIL can be frozen by anyone. You can verify this on the same Solscan token page — look for "Freeze Authority: Revoked".

What is the contract address?

The TrustTails mint address (contract address) on Solana is:

Always verify addresses on-chain. If you see a different address promoted anywhere — in a DM, a Telegram group, a tweet, or a website — it is a scam. There is only one official contract.

BUYING & TRADING

Current status and how to participate

Can I buy TAIL right now?

No. TrustTails is currently in pre-launch. There is no active liquidity pool on Raydium or any other DEX, and no DexScreener listing yet. TAIL is not yet tradeable. Announcing the launch is something we will do through official channels only — X (@trusttailscoin), the Telegram announcements channel, and this website. If anyone claims you can buy TAIL right now, they are running a scam.

How will I be able to buy TAIL when trading opens?

When a liquidity pool is created, TAIL will be purchasable using a Solana-compatible wallet (such as Phantom or Solflare) on a decentralised exchange — most likely Raydium. The process is: fund a wallet with SOL, visit the DEX, connect your wallet, and swap SOL for TAIL using the official contract address to find the correct pool.

We will publish a step-by-step guide on our How to Buy page before trading opens. That page will be the authoritative reference. Do not rely on third-party tutorials until we link to them.

See our Is it legit? page for guidance on verifying any listing is genuine before transacting.

Verification, scams, and honesty

  • Mint authority: revoked (visible on Solscan — no new tokens can be minted)

We also encourage you to read the Transparency page and our Is it legit? explainer, which walks through exactly how to read the on-chain data and what to look for in any token you consider.

How do I know this isn't a rug pull or scam?

We can't force you to trust us — and that's intentional. Trust should be built on verifiable evidence, not on a team asking you to believe them. Here is what you can verify right now, independently, without taking our word for anything:

  • Mint authority is revoked — the team cannot print new tokens
  • Freeze authority is revoked — the team cannot lock your wallet
  • Supply is fixed at 1,000,000,000 TAIL with no hidden allocation mechanism

What we cannot prove in advance: future behaviour, ongoing commitment, or that the project will reach any of its stated goals. That's true of every project. What we can do — and are doing — is make every claim checkable. The whitepaper explains the distribution model in full.

Warning signs common in scam tokens that TrustTails does not have: secret mint authority, freeze authority, anonymous promised returns, fake partnership claims, or a fake presale before a DEX pool exists.

VISION & FUTURE

Where TrustTails is heading

What is the long-term vision for TrustTails?

TrustTails is structured in three phases. Phase 1 is community-building — this is live now. The goal is to grow a genuine community before trading begins, because a token with no community is just a number on a spreadsheet.

Phase 2 is independent infrastructure — exploratory work around tooling, utility, and ecosystem presence. No specifics are confirmed yet.

Phase 3 is a long-term, explicitly unconfirmed direction toward finance-sector relevance. This is an aspiration, not a current reality.
