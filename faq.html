What is TAIL Network?

TAIL Network is an independent, application-specific, sovereign Layer-1 blockchain being built using the Cosmos SDK — an open-source framework for building blockchains that operate entirely on their own, rather than as tokens on another network.

An independent Layer-1 means this project controls its own chain: its own validators, its own governance process, its own upgrade path, and its own economic design. This is fundamentally different from launching an ERC-20 on Ethereum or an SPL token on Solana, where you are operating within another network's rules and infrastructure.

Cosmos SDK chains can also opt into the IBC protocol (Inter-Blockchain Communication), enabling them to interoperate with other Cosmos-ecosystem chains. Sovereignty and interoperability are the two primary reasons a project might choose this path.

Current status: in development. TAIL Network is not live. There is no mainnet, no chain explorer, and no staking today. We will not invent timelines or feature promises. Follow @trusttailscoin and the official Telegram for verified development updates.

What is the Cosmos SDK and why does it matter for TAIL Network?

The Cosmos SDK is an open-source software framework for building independent, application-specific blockchains. Rather than deploying a smart contract on top of another network's infrastructure, a Cosmos SDK chain is its own standalone blockchain — with its own block producers (validators), its own governance, its own upgrade process, and its own economic parameters.

This is called a sovereign Layer-1. Well-known Cosmos SDK projects include the Cosmos Hub (ATOM), Osmosis, dYdX, and many others in the broader Cosmos ecosystem.

For TAIL Network, building on the Cosmos SDK means the project is not dependent on Solana, Ethereum, or any other third-party network's fees, congestion, governance decisions, or upgrade cycles. The chain can be designed specifically for its community's requirements.

Cosmos SDK chains can also connect to each other — and to other compatible networks — via the IBC protocol (Inter-Blockchain Communication), enabling asset transfers and messaging between chains without sacrificing independence.

TAIL Network is in development. Understanding the technology gives useful context for the vision, but none of this represents a promise about specific features, timelines, or outcomes. See TAIL Network →

What does "mint authority revoked" and "freeze authority revoked" actually mean?

On Solana, SPL tokens can optionally carry two special administrative powers:

Mint authority — the power to create (mint) additional tokens after the initial deployment. If retained, a project could inflate supply at any time, diluting existing holders. When mint authority is revoked, the total supply is permanently fixed. No one — not the deployer, not the team, not any future owner — can ever mint another $TAIL token.

Freeze authority — the power to freeze a specific token account, preventing that wallet from sending or receiving the token. When freeze authority is revoked, no wallet holding $TAIL can ever be frozen or restricted by anyone. Holders retain full control of their tokens.

Both revocations are permanent, one-way, on-chain actions. Verify them yourself on Solscan — look for "Mint Authority: None" and "Freeze Authority: None" in the token overview.
