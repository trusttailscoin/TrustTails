What each parameter actually means

Three on-chain facts. Here is why each one matters for anyone holding TAIL.

Fixed Supply — 1,000,000,000 TAIL

One billion tokens were minted at launch. That is the entire supply — no vesting drip, no reserved inflation schedule. The number you see on Solscan today is the number that will exist in ten years. Dilution via new issuance is structurally impossible.

Mint Authority Revoked

On Solana, a token's mint authority is the private key permission that allows new tokens to be printed. Once revoked, it cannot be reassigned or recovered — not by the founding team, not by anyone. Minting additional TAIL is permanently and provably off the table.

Freeze Authority Revoked

Freeze authority is the ability to lock a specific wallet so it cannot send or receive a token. With this authority revoked, no wallet holding TAIL can ever be frozen — not by the team, not by any technical upgrade. Your tokens remain in your control.

Tokenomics FAQ

Does "fixed supply" mean price will go up?
No. Fixed supply means no new tokens can be minted — that is a structural property, not a price guarantee. Price is determined by market demand, liquidity, and many factors outside our control. Cryptocurrency markets are highly volatile and TAIL could lose all value. Nothing here is financial advice.
What exactly does revoking mint authority do on Solana?
On Solana's SPL token standard, each token can have an optional mint authority — a keypair that controls whether new tokens can be issued. When this authority is set to null (revoked), no new tokens can ever be minted, regardless of who holds the original deployer wallet. The revocation is a permanent, irreversible on-chain state change you can inspect directly on Solscan.
What does revoking freeze authority protect me from?
Without freeze authority revoked, a token issuer could theoretically lock any holder's wallet — preventing them from transferring or selling their tokens. Revoking this authority removes that capability entirely. No wallet holding TAIL can be frozen by any party, including the founding team. This is verifiable on-chain as a null freeze authority in the token metadata.
Why don't you publish a team allocation or treasury percentage?
We have not published a breakdown because we will not publish numbers we cannot back up on-chain in a way you can independently verify. What you can see right now is total supply and current holder distribution on Solscan. We believe a falsified pie chart is more harmful than an honest "we haven't published that yet." If a verifiable allocation breakdown becomes available, it will appear here.
Where can I buy TAIL?
TrustTails is currently pre-launch. There is no active trading pool and TAIL is not yet available to purchase through any legitimate venue. Any offer to sell you TAIL right now is a scam. We will announce the launch through our official X account and Telegram announcements channel.
Has the contract been audited?
We have not commissioned or received a formal smart contract audit. We will not claim one until it exists and can be publicly referenced. The on-chain verifiable facts — fixed supply, revoked mint authority, revoked freeze authority — are structural protections that anyone can verify independently without relying on an audit firm's report.
