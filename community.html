How We Roll

Community values and ground rules

There is no moderation army and no rigid rulebook. Just a short list of things that keep the conversation worth having.

We value

  • Honest questions. There are no dumb questions here, including "is this a rug?" Ask it. A community that can't handle scrutiny doesn't deserve trust.
  • Respectful disagreement. Sceptics and believers can coexist. Challenge ideas, not people.
  • Verified sources only. When sharing information about TrustTails, link to official channels or on-chain data. Speculation is fine — just label it as such.
  • Reporting scammers. If you see a fake presale, an impersonator, or an unsolicited DM, flag it in the group. That's a community service.
  • DYOR culture. Read the whitepaper, check the tokenomics, verify the contract on Solscan. Encourage others to do the same.

We don't allow

  • Price predictions and moon talk. "100x by end of week" helps no one and misleads newcomers. Keep it grounded.
  • Coordinated shilling. Do not spam other groups, forums, or comment sections to promote TAIL. It erodes trust faster than it builds it.
  • Fake presale links or "early allocation" offers. TrustTails has no presale. Anyone offering one is not affiliated with this project.
  • Unverified partnership claims. TrustTails has no confirmed partnerships. Do not speculate about institutions or companies as if a deal exists.
  • Harassment or bad-faith FUD. Criticism is welcome. Targeted abuse and deliberate misinformation are not.
Why It Matters

No paid influencers. No sponsored hype.

This is a deliberate choice, not a budget constraint.

What paid promotion actually signals

When a project pays influencers to promote a token pre-launch, it means two things: the team is willing to mislead about organic interest, and early holders may be positioned to exit while latecomers buy in on manufactured excitement. That pattern has played out countless times. TrustTails was designed with that in mind.

Why grassroots trust has a different texture

When someone shares TrustTails because they genuinely found it interesting — not because they were paid — that carries different weight. Grassroots communities are slower to grow and harder to fake. That friction is the point. The thesis is that credibility has to be earned, not purchased. The community is the first test of that.

If you share TrustTails with others, share the contract address, the whitepaper, and the on-chain verification link — not promises. Let people form their own view. That is the only kind of growth worth having.
