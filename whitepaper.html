Why revoke mint authority?

A live mint authority means whoever holds it can create new tokens at any time, inflating supply and diluting every holder. By revoking mint authority on-chain, TrustTails made the 1 billion supply permanent. No one — including the founders — can mint more TAIL. Ever.

Why revoke freeze authority?

Freeze authority lets a token issuer lock individual wallets — preventing holders from moving or selling their tokens. Revoking it means no wallet can ever be frozen by the project. Your tokens remain yours. Verify this on Solscan under the token's "Authorities" section.

Part 2 — TAIL Network

An independent blockchain — in development

TAIL Network is a sovereign Layer-1 blockchain being built on the Cosmos SDK. It is not live. Understanding what that means — and why the project chose this path — is the core technical argument of the whitepaper.

What is the Cosmos SDK?

The Cosmos SDK is an open-source framework for building independent, application-specific blockchains. Unlike deploying a token on an existing chain, building with the Cosmos SDK means creating a wholly separate network with its own validators, consensus mechanism, block times, governance, and economics. The result is often called a "sovereign blockchain" — the project controls the chain rather than being a tenant on someone else's network.

Cosmos SDK chains can communicate with each other and with other IBC-compatible networks via the Inter-Blockchain Communication protocol. This is an existing, production-grade protocol — not a promise.

Why build an independent L1?

The whitepaper explains the reasoning without overpromising outcomes. An application-specific, sovereign chain gives a project complete control over its upgrade path, fee structure, governance model, and the economics of the native token. It cannot be altered, deprecated, or repriced by a third-party chain operator.

That sovereignty comes with real cost and real risk: a new chain must earn its own validators, liquidity, and ecosystem. The whitepaper is explicit about this. Building an independent L1 is harder, slower, and less certain than launching a token on an existing network. The case for it must be made by results, not by claims. TAIL Network is in development — not complete.

The Cosmos SDK is an open-source framework for building independent, sovereign, application-specific blockchains.
